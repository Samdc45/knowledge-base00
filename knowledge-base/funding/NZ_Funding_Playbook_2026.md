# New Zealand Deep Tech Funding Playbook 2026
## Strategic Guide for TruPeg and CivilApp Glasses

The New Zealand innovation funding landscape underwent a massive restructuring in 2025 with the disestablishment of Callaghan Innovation. Government funding for Research and Development (R&D) has been consolidated under the Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE) Innovation Services [1]. Despite these changes, substantial capital remains available for deep tech, hardware, and construction technology startups.

This playbook outlines the exact funding pathways, eligibility criteria, and a step-by-step execution strategy to fund the development of TruPeg (underground asset mapping) and CivilApp Glasses (AR wearables for construction).

---

## 1. Non-Dilutive Government Grants (MBIE)

With Callaghan Innovation gone, MBIE Innovation Services now administers the primary grant pathways for early-stage companies [2]. These grants are non-dilutive, meaning you do not give up any equity in your company.

### New to R&D Grant
This is the ideal starting point for funding the initial prototyping of TruPeg or the CivilApp Glasses. It is designed for businesses that are new to structured R&D.

| Detail | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Funding Amount** | 40% co-funding up to $400,000 NZD [3] |
| **Your Contribution** | 60% of the project costs [3] |
| **Duration** | Up to 2 years [3] |
| **Eligibility** | Must be a registered NZ company, have spent less than $150k on R&D in the past 3 years, and received less than $5k in government R&D funding [3]. |
| **Key Requirement** | Must aim to resolve a scientific or technological uncertainty where a competent professional cannot currently answer the question [2]. At least 5% of costs must go toward capability development (e.g., IP training, project management) [3]. |

**Application Strategy:** Position the CivilApp Glasses or TruPeg not just as software development, but as solving a core technological uncertainty (e.g., integrating precise spatial mapping sensors into ruggedised, low-power wearable hardware for civil environments).

### R&D Tax Incentive (RDTI)
Once you graduate from the New to R&D Grant and are spending more consistently on development, the RDTI becomes your primary mechanism for recovering costs.

| Detail | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Benefit** | 15% tax credit on eligible R&D expenditure [4] |
| **Threshold** | Minimum eligible R&D spend of $50,000 per year [4] |
| **Mechanism** | Reduces your income tax bill, or can be paid out in cash if your company is in a tax loss position (which is typical for early-stage hardware startups) [4]. |

## 2. Deep Tech Incubators & Venture Capital

For hardware and deep tech like TruPeg, venture capital that understands long development cycles is critical. New Zealand has a specific ecosystem for this.

### WNT Ventures
WNT Ventures is New Zealand's most established deep tech investor and incubator, frequently acting as the first institutional money into a company [5]. They specialise in advanced manufacturing, sensing, and automation—perfectly aligning with TruPeg and CivilApp [6].

| Detail | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Investment Stage** | Pre-seed to Seed. They often invest before there is a validated proof of concept or revenue [6]. |
| **Pre-Incubation** | Access to a $35,000 grant to explore commercial viability, validate the market, and build an IP strategy before formal investment [6]. |
| **Incubation Loan** | Access to a $750,000 repayable loan (formerly the Callaghan loan, now managed through the incubator system) [6]. |
| **Process** | Initial meeting → Due Diligence / Pre-incubation → Investment Committee Pitch → Term Sheet [6]. |

### New Zealand Growth Capital Partners (NZGCP)
NZGCP is a government-backed entity designed to stimulate early-stage investment [7]. They do not lead rounds, but they co-invest alongside lead investors like WNT Ventures or angel groups.

| Fund | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Scout Fund (New in 2026)** | A pre-seed fund designed to write the very first commercialisation cheques for promising startups [8]. |
| **Aspire NZ Seed Fund** | Co-invests directly into early-stage tech startups at the proof of concept, seed, and early expansion stages [7]. |
| **Elevate NZ Venture Fund** | A $300m fund of funds that invests into Series A and B rounds [7]. |

## 3. Regional and Export Support

### Regional Business Partner Network (RBPN)
Before applying for major grants, you must register with the RBPN. Your local partner acts as a gateway to MBIE funding and can provide vouchers to pay for professional services [9].

*   **Action:** Register southconsultants.biz on the RBPN portal immediately. It takes 5 minutes and requires your NZBN [10].
*   **Benefit:** Access to the Management Capability Fund, which provides up to 50% co-funding (capped at $5,000) to hire experts for business planning, capital raising strategy, or marketing [9].

### New Zealand Trade and Enterprise (NZTE)
When TruPeg and CivilApp are ready for the APAC market (Australia, Singapore, etc.), NZTE provides export support.

*   **Beachheads Programme:** Connects you with private sector advisors in your target markets.
*   **International Growth Fund:** Co-funding for specific international market development projects once you have established domestic traction.

## Recommended Execution Roadmap for 2026

1.  **Month 1: RBPN Registration.** Register with the Regional Business Partner Network to unlock capability vouchers. Use a voucher to hire a grant writer or IP strategist.
2.  **Month 2: WNT Ventures Outreach.** Contact WNT Ventures for an initial chat about TruPeg and CivilApp. Aim to enter their Pre-Incubation phase to secure the $35k validation grant.
3.  **Month 3: MBIE New to R&D Grant.** Apply for the New to R&D Grant to fund 40% of the initial hardware prototyping and sensor integration for TruPeg.
4.  **Month 6-9: Seed Round.** Leverage the progress from the MBIE grant and WNT pre-incubation to pitch the WNT Investment Committee for a formal seed round, bringing in NZGCP's Aspire fund as a co-investor.
5.  **Month 12+: RDTI.** Register for the 15% R&D Tax Incentive to recover costs on all ongoing development.
