# Global Funding and Grant Options for Deep Tech Construction Startups (2026)

**Focus:** TruPeg (Underground Asset Mapping) and CivilApp Glasses (AR Wearables for Civil Construction)
**Regions:** New Zealand, APAC, and Europe
**Prepared by:** Manus AI

This report outlines the most viable non-dilutive funding, R&D tax incentives, and deep tech co-investment schemes available in 2026 across New Zealand, the broader APAC region, and Europe. It is specifically tailored for hardware-enabled SaaS, AR wearables, and underground mapping technologies in the civil construction sector (ConTech/Deep Tech).

---

## 1. New Zealand Funding Landscape (Post-Callaghan Reform)

Following the disestablishment of Callaghan Innovation in early 2025, New Zealand's innovation funding has been restructured under the Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE) through "Innovation Services" [1]. The focus remains heavily on R&D tax incentives and early-stage commercialisation.

### 1.1. Research and Development Tax Incentive (RDTI)
The RDTI is now the primary mechanism for funding ongoing technology development in New Zealand. It provides a **15% tax credit** on eligible R&D expenditure [1]. For TruPeg and CivilApp, eligible activities include software development (subject to a $25 million cap for internal purposes, but core to commercial products) and hardware prototyping [2] [3]. 

> "The RDTI is about encouraging R&D that pushes beyond existing scientific and technological barriers, and does this in a way that’s both systematic and with a specific goal in mind." [1]

### 1.2. New to R&D Grant
Administered by MBIE Innovation Services, this grant supports startups at the beginning of their R&D journey. It is designed to help companies establish in-house R&D capabilities and de-risk early technology development [1]. This is highly suitable for funding the initial prototyping phases of CivilApp Glasses or the AI models for TruPeg.

### 1.3. Deep Tech Incubators and Angel Networks
New Zealand's deep tech investment sector saw a 21.6% surge in 2025 [4]. While direct government grants have shifted, co-funding through deep tech incubators remains available. Previously under Callaghan (now transitioned), these programs offered up to **$1 million in funding** ($750,000 as a repayable grant) to commercialise complex technologies [5]. Additionally, local angel networks are increasingly focused on PropTech and ConTech [6].

## 2. APAC Regional Funding Options

The APAC region offers aggressive funding for deep tech and construction technology, particularly in markets looking to modernise infrastructure and improve site safety.

### 2.1. Australia
Australia is heavily investing in commercialising research and supporting tech scale-ups.

*   **R&D Tax Incentive:** Tech startups with a June 30 year-end must register by April 30, 2026, to claim up to a **43.5% refundable tax offset** for eligible R&D activities [7]. The ATO is scrutinising claims closely in 2026, so robust documentation of the AR development or mapping algorithms is essential [8].
*   **CSIRO ON Accelerate:** A free, three-month commercialisation program for research-driven ventures, providing skills, networks, and industry engagement [9].
*   **Austrade Landing Pads:** While not direct cash grants, this program provides crucial market-entry support, education, and connections for Australian tech businesses expanding internationally (e.g., into Indonesia or Singapore) [10].

### 2.2. Singapore
Singapore is positioning itself as the premier hub for deep tech and ConTech in Southeast Asia, with substantial government backing.

*   **Startup SG Tech:** Specifically targets early-stage companies developing proprietary technology or strong intellectual property. It funds Proof of Concept (POC) and Proof of Value (POV) work, making it ideal for validating TruPeg's mapping accuracy or CivilApp's AR capabilities [11].
*   **Enterprise Development Grant (EDG):** Supports projects that help businesses upgrade, innovate, grow, and transform, funding up to 50% of qualifying costs [12].
*   **Startup SG Equity:** A co-investment scheme where the government co-invests alongside private investors in deep tech startups, addressing early-stage funding gaps [13].

### 2.3. South Korea
South Korea has launched an unprecedented funding initiative for deep tech in 2026.

*   **TIPS (Tech Incubator Program for Startup):** The 2026 TIPS program has expanded its budget significantly. It pairs government R&D grants with private investment. The number of supported startups is increasing, with a strong focus on deep tech and ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) [14] [15].
*   **National Startup Era Initiative:** The government has dedicated over $40 billion annually to support entrepreneurship, targeting 10,000 AI and deep tech startups [16] [17].

### 2.4. Japan
Japan's New Energy and Industrial Technology Development Organization (NEDO) actively funds deep tech.

*   **Deep-Tech Startups Support Program (DTSU):** Provides substantial R&D subsidies (sometimes up to 800 million yen) to startups committed to high-risk, innovative technologies [18] [19]. This is highly relevant for the hardware and AI integration required for AR wearables.

## 3. European Funding (Horizon Europe & EIC)

Europe offers some of the largest non-dilutive funding instruments globally for deep tech scale-ups.

### 3.1. European Innovation Council (EIC) Accelerator
The EIC Accelerator is the flagship EU funding program for scale-ups (TRL 6-8). In 2026, it has a budget of €414 million [20].

*   **Funding Structure:** Offers up to **€2.5 million in non-dilutive grants** and up to **€10 million in equity investment** (blended finance) [20].
*   **2026 Updates:** The program now has six cut-off dates per year and a simplified Step 1 application process. It includes dedicated budgets for strategic challenges, though deep tech ConTech fits well within the "Open" category [20].
*   **Relevance:** Highly suitable for TruPeg once the technology is proven and ready for rapid scaling across European infrastructure markets.

### 3.2. Eurostars (EUREKA Network)
Co-funded by Horizon Europe, Eurostars targets R&D-intensive SMEs collaborating across borders.

*   **Funding:** Provides €300,000 to €500,000 per partner for collaborative R&D projects (TRL 3+) [20].
*   **Pathway to EIC:** Successful Eurostars projects can bypass Step 1 of the EIC Accelerator and proceed directly to Step 2, making it an excellent stepping stone [20].

### 3.3. Innovate UK (United Kingdom)
Innovate UK is the national innovation agency, offering various grants to turn breakthrough technologies into scalable businesses [21]. They frequently run sector-specific competitions that cover construction technology, smart infrastructure, and digital twins (highly relevant for TruPeg).

### 3.4. National EU Programmes (Germany & Netherlands)
*   **Germany (EXIST & KfW):** Offers the EXIST Startup Grant (up to €30,000 for materials + stipends) for university spinouts, and the KfW Future Fund for Series A-C co-investment [20].
*   **Netherlands (WBSO):** Primarily utilizes the WBSO R&D tax credit, offering up to a 50% tax credit on R&D wages, with extra supplements for starter companies in 2026 [20].

## Strategic Recommendations for South Consultants

1.  **Immediate Term (NZ/AU Base):** Maximise the **RDTI (15% in NZ or up to 43.5% in AU)** to subsidise the core software and hardware development of TruPeg and CivilApp. Apply for the **New to R&D Grant** (NZ) to fund initial prototyping.
2.  **Medium Term (APAC Expansion):** Leverage **Startup SG Tech** in Singapore to fund Proof of Value deployments of TruPeg in a highly regulated, infrastructure-dense market.
3.  **Long Term (Global Scaling):** Prepare for the **EIC Accelerator** or **Eurostars** by forming a cross-border R&D partnership (e.g., testing TruPeg with a European utility company) to access multi-million Euro scaling capital.
